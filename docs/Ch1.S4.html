1.4.1 Cyclic Shifts Property of Trace with Hadamard Products

The circular shift property of trace, i.e.,

trace⁡(𝑾1⁢𝑾2⁢𝑾3)=trace⁡(𝑾3⁢𝑾1⁢𝑾2)=trace⁡(𝑾2⁢𝑾3⁢𝑾1),

holds for conventional matrix products of matrices 𝐖1, 𝐖2, and 𝐖3. In the following theorem, we consider circular shifts of matrices involving Hadamard products, e.g., trace⁡(𝐖1⁢(𝐖2∘𝐖3)⁢𝐖4).

Theorem 5.

Let m1,m2,m3>0 and let 𝐖1∈ℂm1×m2, 𝐖2,𝐖3∈ℂm2×m3, and 𝐖4∈ℂm3×m1 be arbitrary matrices. Then,

trace⁡(𝑾1⁢(𝑾2∘𝑾3)⁢𝑾4)=trace⁡((𝑾2T∘(𝑾4⁢𝑾1))⁢𝑾3). (1.8)
Proof.

We have

trace⁡(𝑾1⁢(𝑾2∘𝑾3)⁢𝑾4) =trace⁡((𝑾2∘𝑾3)⁢𝑾4⁢𝑾1)
=∑i=1m2∑j=1m3[𝑾2]i,j⁢[𝑾3]i,j⁢[𝑾4⁢𝑾1]j,i
=∑j=1m3∑i=1m2[𝑾2T]j,i⁢[𝑾4⁢𝑾1]j,i⁢[𝑾3]i,j
=trace⁡((𝑾2T∘(𝑾4⁢𝑾1))⁢𝑾3). (1.9)

∎
